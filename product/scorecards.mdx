## What are scorecards?

Scorecards evaluate rep performance on individual calls by scoring a set of elements — things like "discovery questions asked," "competitive positioning," or "next steps confirmed." Each element gets a color-coded score with coaching feedback.

## How scoring works

Each element in a scorecard is scored on a three-point scale:

| Score | Meaning | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **G** (Green) | Thorough | The rep covered this element well |
| **Y** (Yellow) | Partial | The rep touched on it but missed key aspects |
| **R** (Red) | Missed | The element was not addressed |

For each score, the AI provides:
- **Rationale** — why it gave that score, with direct quotes from the call
- **Suggestion** — specific coaching advice for improvement

## Templates

Scorecards are powered by templates — customizable evaluation frameworks that define which elements to score and in what order.

Templates can be configured at multiple levels:
- **System-level** — default templates available to all teams
- **Org-level** — custom templates for your organization
- **Team-level** — team-specific templates

Templates go through a versioning lifecycle: **draft** → **sandbox** (testing) → **main** (active) → **archived**.

## Viewing scorecards

Scorecards appear as [posts](/product/posts) in your feed. You can:
- Filter by call type, date range, or team member
- Click into any scorecard to see element-by-element scores
- Review the AI's rationale and coaching suggestions for each element

## Multi-pass scoring

For deeper evaluation, scorecards can use multi-pass scoring — focusing on fewer elements per pass to provide more detailed analysis. This trades speed for depth.
